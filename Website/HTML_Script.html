The goal of VIntDef (Visualising Intelligence Definitions) is to get a better understanding of intelligence. For that purpose several definitions of intelligence were collected and visualised. The Definitions were taken from a survey from the Artificial General Intelligence Sentinel Initiative (you can reach their website over the link in the menu) and the paper “A collection of Definitions of Intelligence” from Shane Legg and Marcus Hutter from 2007. The definitions are splitted into four different collections, the 71 from the paper, 34 definition that were part of the AGISI survey, 125 definitions of human intelligence and 213 definitions of machine intelligence, so that a total of 443 definitions are analyzed. Two types of visualization are used. One is a graph, the other a chart. You can filter the definitions by clicking the top hexagon. The graph consists of nodes and edges. Each node represents a word. The bigger the node is the more often the word appears in the chosen definitions. The edges connect the nodes which stand next to each other in a definition. The thicker the edge is the more often the words appear next to each other. The chart shows the number of occurence per word.
